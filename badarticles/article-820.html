Friday, 16 September 2011.
Posted By :Vantage FX on:16/09/2011 3:27:00 PM
AUD, USDJPY, USD, EUR, Euro

Overall the currency markets have settled down after last night’s news of the ECB among other central banks adding liquidity to the European banks. The Asian equity markets have all seen this as positive with the major makes up in trading.

After the EURUSD jumped above 1.39 for the first time in weeks, it has stabilized around the mid 1.38’s throughout today trade.

The AUDUSD has continued to move higher in the Asian session, reflecting the risk on trade as investors continue to seek higher yielding assets, and is currently trading at 1.0341 The NZDUSD has also climbed higher up 0.2% to 0.8251.

Yields on Japan’s two-year notes stood at 0.13%, compared with 0.19% in the U.S. The Asian nation’s 10- year yields were little changed at 1% today. The yen has remained weaker than 76 per dollar since it reached 75.95 on Aug. 19, a post-World War II record high, while it strengthened to a 10-year high of 103.90 per euro this week(EURJPY Chart Above).  

A stronger yen decreases the value of overseas earnings for the nation’s exporters and makes Japanese-made products costlier overseas. Standard & Poor’s cut its forecast this week for Japan’s economic growth in 2011 to about zero, saying the yen may undermine a recovery from a record earthquake in March.

There is a lack of news out tonight and traders may look towards the University of Michigan confidence figures for some guidance, unless further unexpected news is release on overnight.
