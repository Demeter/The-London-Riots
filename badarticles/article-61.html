Dollar Index Holding Strong Gains
09-Sep-11 14:51 ET
10-Yr: +16/32..1.918%.. USD/JPY: 77.41.. EUR/USD: 1.3676
Strong Gains: The dollar index is climbing back towards its best levels of the session as traders prepare for the final hour of trading in U.S. equities for the week. Worries of a Greek default have riled markets today with EURUSD coming under heavy selling pressure as traders look to cut their exposure to the single currency ahead of the weekend. Word that Germany was working on a framework to protect its banks in the event of a Greek default dropped the single currency to its worst levels of the session, to down more than 255 pips, but it has since pared its losses and is lower by 220 pips at 1.3665. Greece's Finance Ministry has come out and denied the default speculation, but traders remain skeptical as they have seen this song and dance before. The G7 finance ministers are meeting this weekend in France, but little progress towards a resolution is expected to be made. ECB President Jean-Claude Trichet will speak in Basel at the Bank of International Settlements on Sunday. GBPUSD is lower by 95 pips at 1.5865 and has breached important 1.59 support. With today's breakdown a move into the mid 1.50s appears likely unless previous support can be quickly recaptured. USDCHF is up another 85 pips at .8830 and is now flirting with its 200-day moving average. It would come as no surprise if there was some sort of pullback off these levels with a test of .8600 support being a very distinct possibility. The Swisse failed to see safe-haven flows during today's flight from risk as traders remain worried of Swiss National Bank intervention.



USDJPY ran as high as 77.85 earlier in the session, but saw some heavy selling in response to the Greek worries. Those seeking safety rushed into the yen as fear of Bank of Japan/Ministry of Finance intervention remains far less than that of the Swiss National Bank. The pair was able to trade through its 77.50 resistance, but has since slipped back below that level. Japan's BSI Manufacturing Index, Tertiary Industry Activity, and Monetary Policy Meeting Minutes will all be released Sunday evening. AUDUSD has come under heavy selling pressure today as traders dumped the commodity-based currency in favor of safe-havens. Today's selling has the pair down 135 pips near 1.0440 and ready to test 1.04 support that is aided by the 200-day moving average. Australia's trade balance will be released late Sunday. Chinese markets will be close on Monday for the Mid-Autumn Festival.

USDCAD is on pace for its best close since January 31 with today's 75 pip gain. Buyers have run the pair up to .9965 and have given it a chance to close above parity for the first time since late January.
