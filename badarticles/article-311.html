Rupee spirals down against dollar

Lokeshwarri S.K.

September 13, 2011:  

It was a period of heightened volatility in foreign exchange markets since last Friday. Matters came to a head in the Euro zone with fears of an imminent Greek sovereign debt default pushing prices of Greek bonds to record lows. This in turn raised a question mark over French banks that are holding these bonds. The euro went spiralling lower and the dollar went shooting higher on rising risk-aversion.

Rupee crashed 3 per cent lower from Thursday's close of 46.19 to Tuesday's low of 47.6 as the sluice gates of selling were thrown open. Downward reversal in equity markets and FIIs pulling out about Rs 1,300 crore since last Friday also affected the sentiment adversely. However, according to EPFR Global, a global fund-flow tracker, “India Equity Funds, saw outflows ebb to an eight week low as speculation mounted that the Reserve Bank of India will pause its current tightening cycle.”

The one-month rupee forwards in the non-deliverable forward market were trading at 47.9 while three-month forwards were trading at 48.2 reflecting the current bearish sentiment.

Dollar-rupee outlook: The pace of decline in rupee against the dollar over the last three sessions has been breath-taking. The USD-INR pair penetrated the support at 46.3 that we have been watching over the last couple of weeks, with ease and has already moved close to the next support band between 47.1 and 47.7 indicated in our last column.

Technical levels are not sacrosanct when selling fury is unleashed. But as discussed earlier, the zone between 47.1 and 47.7 that halted the decline in May 2010 can arrest the current decline. Another rebound from this zone will mean that the uptrend from March 2009 trough of 52.1 continues to be strong.

However sustained decline below this zone will take the currency pair to 48 or 49 over the ensuing weeks. Long-term view will turn negative on a close below 49 and the next target will be the pair's life-time low of 52.1. The region between 46.2 and 46.3 will be the key short-term resistance in the days ahead.

USD-INR futures moved beyond our outermost target at 46.87 to reach the high of 47.7 on Tuesday. Immediate targets for this contract are 48 and 48.4. The ceiling of the gap formed on Monday at 46.90 can act as the stop-loss for short-term traders. Subsequent supports are at 46.7 and 46.4.

EUR-INR futures: Rupee has been appreciating against the Euro and the EUR-INR futures have been declining as a result. Resistances for the days ahead would be at 65.6 and 66.9. Failure to move above the first resistance will mean that the contract can move down to 63.6 or 63 again.

GBP-INR futures moved in line with our expectation bouncing to 75.49 on Tuesday. Next target for the contract is the previous peak at 76. If this level is breached, the contract can move on to 76.8. Traders can hold the contract with the stop at 74.7. Next support is at 74.3.

JPY-INR futures: Rupee moved lower against the yen causing the JPY-INR futures to take off sky-wards to achieve our first medium-term target at 61. Next medium-term target stays at 66. Supports for the week ahead are at 60.4 and 59.4.
