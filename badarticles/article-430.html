Wednesday, 14 September 2011.
Posted By :Vantage FX on:14/09/2011 3:24:00 PM
AUD, AUDUSD, forex, Asian


European debt concerns continue to dominate market sentiment today, weighing heavily on riskier currencies such as the AUDUSD, which has been sold off from a high of 1.0370, to a low of about 1.0210. The fundamental outlook for the Australian Dollar has deteriorated further, with housing starts coming in well below expectations at 4.7% earlier today. 
 
The likelihood that the Reserve Bank of Australia is going to abandon its ‘mildly restrictive’ policy in an effort to balance regional risks continues to mount. We may see its characteristically hawkish tone soften in coming weeks, with Credit Suisse overnight Index swaps indicating that market participants see the RBA cutting by nearly 150bp over the next 12 months. 
 
The euro declined against both the dollar and the yen, with ongoing concerns that Greece’s debt woes will raise borrowing costs for other countries in the region – including Spain, which is due to sell bonds tomorrow.  The dollar held a two-day slide against the yen before reports that may show U.S. retail sales growth moderated in August, adding to signs of a slowing recovery.
 
The AUDJPY cross has lost approximately 1.6% over the course of today’s trading session, opening the pair up for a test of lows seen in August at around 76.50. Downside risks should prevail in the Australasian currencies. Tests of recent lows are a possibility in the AUDUSD and NZDUSD pairs, which are sitting at 0.9925 and 0.7962 respectively.
 
Traders will focus on Retail Sales figures due out of the US later today.
